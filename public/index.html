About Us - From Andrew's Perspective

I met Hannah one fateful Saturday night in April, 2011. I was working late at SOMACentral, a coworking space in San Francisco, on a side project called RandTxt. My project was supposed to be the “Chatroulette for text messages”, but before I started writing code I wanted to talk to some potential users. I wandered the halls looking for other people who were staying late, and found Hannah huddled at her desk working too.

I had seen Hannah before, as I had to pass her desk each day to get to my office, but I had never spoken to her before. So I walked over, sat down next to her, and asked “Hey, do you like to talk to strangers?”. “Why yes I do!” she replied.

We became fast friends due to our shared love of McDonalds and Safeway soup, and we started dating a little over a year later. Over the years, our relationship has grown in so many ways -- from our first hike to Bass Lake, to our (her) first cat (Hermes, in 2012), to our first trip together (to Hawaii! In 2013). We bonded over our shared love of good food, good friends, and pineapples.

Engagement - From Hannah's Perspective

Andrew had invited me on bike ride and picnic dinner, on an otherwise uneventful Wednesday night. Little did I know the bike ride would change our lives forever.

The ride itself started off a bit odd. It was further than we usually went (especially on crummy gBikes) and much smellier due to the flocks of seabirds.

Andrew led us along the bumpy, non-paved road, and had us stop right by the Pelican Nesting View. After taking a whiff, he quickly suggested we continue biking. When we escaped the smell of waterfowl, we stopped and Andrew laid out a picnic blanket and dinner.

We had dinner and I was pretty cold so I began to pack up quickly, but Andrew wasn’t as eager to leave and asked if I would like dessert. My answer was obvious.

He brought out four desserts including two fortune cookies. I grabbed a cookie and cracked it open. It read, “Will you marry me?”

In the briefest second I thought to myself, “This is a weird fortune.” I looked over and Andrew was down on one knee with a box open and in it, a beautiful ring.

I didn’t know what to say so I said the most Hannah thing possible…

“Is this for real?”


“Yes”


”How did you do that?!”


“I don’t think that’s relevant”


“So…??”


“Of course yes!”


When I look back on our engagement, what stands out is the love, joy, humor,and excitement I felt that night. I couldn’t imagine spending the rest of my life with anyone else.
